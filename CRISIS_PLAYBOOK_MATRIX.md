# Crisis Playbook Matrix (Fictional Election Simulation)

Purpose: Standardize response across crisis types while preserving factual integrity, legal safety, and narrative consistency.

## 1) Universal Timeline Framework

### First 30 Minutes

- Confirm incident trigger and open incident channel.
- Freeze speculative publishing.
- Establish known facts vs unknowns.
- Assign single spokesperson owner.
- Publish holding line (if public visibility is high).

### First 3 Hours

- Complete verification pass and severity tier assignment.
- Produce Option A/B/C brief with risk analysis.
- Approve one response line with Message ID.
- Deploy first corrective package across priority channels.
- Launch monitoring dashboard and escalation watch.

### First 24 Hours

- Run repeated monitor-adjust cycles (every 30-60 min initially).
- Deploy validation assets (documents, timeline, third-party checks).
- Address secondary narratives and opponent reframes.
- Issue transparent correction/update log entries.

### Recovery Week

- Publish accountability recap and evidence archive.
- Run after-action review and update playbooks.
- Repair trust through proactive engagement and measurable commitments.

## 2) Crisis Type Matrix

| Crisis Type | Typical Trigger | Primary Risk | First Message Objective | Preferred Response Posture |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Scandal allegation | Leak, accusation, investigative story | Credibility collapse | Acknowledge, verify, avoid overclaiming | Transparent + evidence-led |
| Economic shock | Inflation spike, layoffs, market panic | Competence doubt | Show command, empathy, practical steps | Calm managerial competence |
| Viral out-of-context clip | Edited/misleading video spread | Narrative hijack | Restore context fast with proof | Correct + reframe |
| Candidate gaffe | Live event misstatement | Character judgment and replay risk | Clarify intent without defensiveness | Clarify + reset |
| Security/safety incident | Violence, threat, unrest | Fear escalation, polarization | Prioritize safety and unity | De-escalatory leadership |
| Election process rumor | Voting/counting conspiracy claims | Legitimacy erosion | Defend process facts and institutions | Procedural clarity |

## 3) Decision Tree (High Level)

1. Is the claim verified?
   - No -> hold line + active verification; no speculative details.
   - Yes -> proceed to impact analysis.
2. Is spread velocity high and rising?
   - Yes -> prioritize fast multi-channel correction within 120 minutes.
   - No -> targeted correction and monitor.
3. Is legal exposure material?
   - Yes -> legal-led wording and approval required before full release.
   - No -> comms-led rollout with policy/fact checks.
4. Is core message spine threatened?
   - Yes -> candidate-level alignment and surrogate briefing required.
   - No -> local/channel-level adaptation allowed.

## 4) Response Package Templates

## A) Holding Statement Template

- We are aware of [incident].
- We are verifying facts with [authoritative sources].
- We will provide an update by [time].
- We ask the public to avoid sharing unverified claims.

## B) Verified Correction Template

- Claim circulating: [short description]
- What is verified: [fact 1, fact 2]
- Source links: [link list]
- What happens next: [next step and timeline]

## C) Candidate Statement Template (when needed)

- Acknowledge impact and concern.
- State verified facts only.
- Clarify accountability and corrective action.
- Re-anchor to campaign values and public interest.

## 5) Roles by Crisis Stage

| Stage | Lead | Supporting |
|---|---|---|
| Detect & classify | Data/Intel Lead | Monitoring team |
| Verify & legal check | Fact team + Legal | Policy lead |
| Strategy selection | Purple Manager | Strategy + Comms |
| Message production | Comms Director | Speech/creative |
| Channel deployment | Channel leads | Field + surrogate ops |
| Monitoring & adaptation | Analytics lead | Comms + strategy |
| Postmortem | Operations chief | All workstream leads |

## 6) Escalation Triggers

- Spread velocity > 3x baseline in 20 minutes.
- Mainstream outlet pickup of unverified claim.
- Conflicting internal lines detected.
- Legal challenge notice or takedown dispute.
- Candidate safety or election-process legitimacy risk.

## 7) Do/Do Not Rules

Do:
- Label uncertainty clearly.
- Correct quickly with source-backed facts.
- Keep one Message ID across channels.
- Log all updates and timestamps.

Do Not:
- Guess details under pressure.
- Delete without correction notice.
- Over-promise before verification.
- Use inflammatory framing that deepens polarization.

## 8) Postmortem Checklist (Within 24 Hours)

- Was the first holding line on SLA?
- Did all published assets remain fact-consistent?
- Were any contradictions introduced across channels?
- What was trust delta by key segment?
- What should be changed in playbook, staffing, or tooling?
