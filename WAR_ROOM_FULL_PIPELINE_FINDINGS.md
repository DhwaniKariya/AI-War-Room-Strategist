## Yellow Researcher - Key Findings

1. Credibility shocks move voters faster than policy announcements in low-trust, polarized conditions.
2. The decisive electorate is smaller but highly turnout-elastic, especially distrust-heavy groups.
3. Swing voters are situational risk managers, not consistently centrist by ideology.
4. Misinformation persists most in closed networks where interpersonal trust outranks institutional trust.
5. Fact-checking helps moderates but rarely flips identity-aligned false beliefs in hardened blocs.
6. Opponents exploit timing asymmetry: falsehood spreads instantly while verification lags.
7. Regional grievance narratives amplify national frames and can override central campaign intent.
8. Youth response is legitimacy-driven; fairness concerns can drive abstention or anti-system behavior.
9. Fringe-to-mainstream narrative contagion is structural in current media ecosystems.
10. The largest systemic risk is a trust-collapse cascade (institutional doubt + identity fear + information disorder).

## Red-Orange Designer - Key Findings

1. Triage quality is the core bottleneck; speed without trust scoring causes narrative whiplash.
2. A single source of truth (message house + message IDs) is the strongest anti-contradiction control.
3. Most crisis failures occur at handoffs, so strict owner/SLA discipline is essential.
4. Severity tiers (T1/T2/T3) prevent overreaction to noise and underreaction to true threats.
5. Misinformation response is strongest when concise correction is followed by reframing.
6. Legal and policy checks must be embedded in option design, not appended at publish time.
7. Channel adaptation should vary tone and format only; facts remain invariant.
8. Decision logs with rationale improve continuity across shifts and reduce repeated errors.
9. Daily line-lock rituals reduce message drift under pressure.
10. Mandatory 24-hour postmortems compound organizational learning and response quality.

## Blue Maker - Key Findings

1. A stream-first architecture with hot/cold storage supports real-time decisions without overengineering.
2. Ethics gates must execute before action release, not only as post-hoc reporting.
3. Simulation-safe defaults (human approval + non-live queue) reduce misuse and overreach risk.
4. Feature freshness and confidence controls matter more than model complexity.
5. Immutable signed audit lineage is essential for trust, compliance, and forensic reconstruction.
6. Incident behavior should fail-closed on ethics/audit paths and fail-open only on non-critical telemetry.
7. Scenario simulations must include legal and budget constraints to remain realistic.
8. Drift and data-integrity monitoring are first-class requirements to prevent silent degradation.
9. Explicit schema separation (signals, decisions, ethics assessments) improves accountability.
10. Verification must balance performance, fairness, and recoverability, not optimize only for lift.

## Green Marketer - Key Findings

1. Trust is the primary growth and persuasion multiplier.
2. Fixed core narrative + localized evidence prevents contradiction across segments and regions.
3. High-misinformation regions require dedicated staffing, tooling, and reserve budget.
4. Interactive formats (open Q&A) reduce cynicism more than one-way advertising.
5. Visible corrections build legitimacy better than silent edits or deletion.
6. Multilingual parity checks are necessary to prevent cross-language drift.
7. Community validators transfer trust more effectively than broad-reach influencers.
8. Geo holdout testing is critical to prove incremental impact of offline channels.
9. Weekly accountability recaps convert campaign attention into durable credibility.
10. Reputation safeguards are direct persuasion assets, not only compliance controls.

## Purple Manager Synthesis - Cross-Agent Conclusions

1. The campaign should optimize for trust-adjusted speed, not raw speed.
2. The best architecture is a closed loop: sense -> score -> simulate -> gate -> deploy -> learn.
3. Message discipline depends on one canonical narrative spine with strict version control.
4. Crisis capability requires prebuilt templates, time-boxed SLAs, and single-owner accountability.
5. Every rapid move should include explicit exploitability and backlash analysis.
6. Ethical constraints (truthfulness, fairness, transparency) are strategic necessities, not optional ideals.
7. Auditability and explainability are required to preserve internal coherence and external legitimacy.
8. Winning strategy is not only persuasion; it is trust preservation under adversarial information pressure.
