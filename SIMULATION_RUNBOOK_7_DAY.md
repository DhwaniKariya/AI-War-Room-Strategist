# 7-Day Simulation Runbook (Fictional Election War Room)

Purpose: Stress-test campaign decision quality, speed, consistency, and trust preservation under realistic pressure.

## 1) Runbook Overview

- Duration: 7 consecutive simulation days
- Mode: Real-time injects with incomplete information
- Core loop: sense -> score -> simulate -> gate -> decide -> deploy -> review
- Daily deliverables: decision log, message pack, risk register update, end-of-day after-action note

## 3) Daily Cadence Template

- 08:30-09:00: morning alignment and line lock
- 09:00-12:00: inject window 1 (primary event)
- 12:00-12:20: recalibration checkpoint
- 12:20-16:00: inject window 2 (secondary spread/counter)
- 16:00-16:30: final adjustment cycle
- 16:30-17:00: end-of-day review and score capture

## 4) Inject Schedule (7 Days)

## Day 1 - Baseline Volatility

- Inject A: conflicting polls (national up, swing regions down)
- Inject B: rumor spike in closed messaging groups
- Objective: validate triage discipline and contradiction prevention

## Day 2 - Policy Tradeoff Pressure

- Inject A: cost-of-living policy praised in cities, criticized in rural districts
- Inject B: opponent reframes as anti-family or anti-worker
- Objective: maintain one policy stance with localized proof without drift

## Day 3 - Viral Clip Distortion

- Inject A: edited candidate clip trends rapidly
- Inject B: mainstream pickup before full context is known
- Objective: rapid correction with evidence while avoiding overreach

## Day 4 - Integrity Challenge

- Inject A: allegations about campaign financing transparency
- Inject B: surrogate gives off-message defense
- Objective: restore trust through transparent, legally safe response

## Day 5 - External Shock

- Inject A: sudden economic negative headline (jobs/inflation)
- Inject B: opponent claims only they can restore order
- Objective: competence narrative under high fear and uncertainty

## Day 6 - Security and Polarization Spike

- Inject A: local security incident triggers identity-framed rumors
- Inject B: pressure to adopt inflammatory rhetoric
- Objective: de-escalatory leadership while protecting support

## Day 7 - Election Process Rumor Surge

- Inject A: conspiracy narratives about voting/counting procedures
- Inject B: coordinated bot-like amplification
- Objective: defend process legitimacy with factual, procedural clarity

## 5) Evaluation Criteria and Scoring

Scoring scale: 1 (poor) to 5 (excellent)

| Dimension | Weight (%) | What Good Looks Like |
|---|---:|---|
| Response speed | 20 | Holding line <= 30 min; verified correction <= 120 min |
| Decision quality | 20 | Clear tradeoff analysis and justified option selection |
| Message consistency | 20 | No cross-channel contradiction; single Message ID |
| Trust preservation | 20 | Minimal trust erosion; strong correction acceptance |
| Ethics/legal compliance | 10 | No blocked non-compliant publication attempts |
| Learning quality | 10 | Concrete playbook updates at end of each day |

Total daily score = weighted sum out of 100.

## 7) Controller Rules (Simulation Control Cell)

- Injects must be time-stamped and partially ambiguous.
- New information may invalidate earlier assumptions.
- Opponent behavior should adapt to campaign choices.
- No inject should require unethical action to "win".
- Score based on process quality and trust outcomes, not only narrative dominance.

## 8) End-of-Week Synthesis

At day 7 close, produce:

1. Top 10 recurring failure modes
2. Top 10 strongest practices
3. Updated SLA thresholds (if needed)
4. Revised escalation triggers
5. Priority tooling/process changes for next sprint
