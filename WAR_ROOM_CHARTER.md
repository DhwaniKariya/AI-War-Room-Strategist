# War Room Charter (Fictional Election Simulation)

Date: 2026-03-03
Owner: Purple Manager (Campaign Manager)
Purpose: Run a trust-first, high-speed decision system under uncertainty without contradiction, legal breach, or reputational collapse.

## 1) Mission and Operating Principles

- Mission: Convert noisy signals into coherent campaign decisions across platform, messaging, defense/contrast, and crisis response.
- Principle 1: Trust-adjusted speed over raw speed.
- Principle 2: One message spine, many localized proofs.
- Principle 3: Facts are invariant across channels; only tone and examples adapt.
- Principle 4: Ethics and legality are hard gates, not advisory notes.
- Principle 5: Every major action is auditable.

## 2) Scope

- In scope: signal monitoring, strategy adjustments, messaging deployment, rapid rebuttal, crisis handling, postmortems.
- Out of scope: deliberate disinformation, fabricated evidence, manipulated media, unlawful targeting.

## 3) Roles and Decision Rights

| Role | Primary Responsibility | Can Decide | Must Escalate |
|---|---|---|---|
| Purple Manager (Campaign Manager) | Final orchestration and conflict resolution | T1/T2 strategic choices | Candidate-level reversals, legal uncertainty |
| Strategy Lead | Tradeoff analysis, option framing | T2 tactical options | If options conflict with message spine |
| Comms Director | Message production and channel alignment | Publish plan after gates pass | If facts are incomplete or contradictory |
| Data/Intel Lead | Signal reliability and risk scoring | Priority queue updates | If data quality falls below threshold |
| Policy Lead | Policy consistency and evidence mapping | Policy-language approval | If commitments or numbers change |
| Legal Counsel | Election/compliance review | Veto non-compliant outputs | Any ambiguous legal interpretation |
| Field Lead | Ground execution and local feedback | Regional rollout timing | If local adaptation creates drift |
| Ethics Officer (or delegated gate owner) | Fairness and manipulation-risk controls | Block unsafe recommendations | Any repeated gate violations |

## 4) Cadence

- Morning alignment (30 min): top risks, line lock, resource allocation.
- Midday recalibration (20 min): signal updates and tactical shifts.
- Evening close (20 min): narrative audit, next-day prep, unresolved risks.
- Live incident mode: 15-minute cycles until stability.

## 5) Severity Model

| Tier | Definition | Initial SLA |
|---|---|---|
| T1 | Existential/high-velocity crisis (legal bombshell, violent incident, viral fabricated clip) | Detection to first holding line <= 30 min |
| T2 | Major attack or narrative distortion with spread potential | Initial corrective package <= 120 min |
| T3 | Routine controversy/noise | Monitor and respond in normal cycle |

## 6) Quality Gates (Must Pass)

1. Signal Gate: two-source corroboration or one high-trust primary source.
2. Decision Gate: option memo includes upside, downside, exploitability, and stop condition.
3. Message Gate: factual, policy, legal, and ethics checks complete.
4. Consistency Gate: all channels reference same Message ID and approved claims.
5. Learning Gate: postmortem filed within 24 hours.

## 7) Escalation Rules

- Escalate immediately to Purple Manager for: election integrity allegations, candidate safety risk, legal injunction risk, message conflict.
- Escalate to Candidate for: apology/admission, core promise reversal, debate posture changes within 24 hours.
- Escalate to legal external counsel for: defamation exposure, takedown disputes, election-law ambiguity.

## 8) Stop-the-Line Rule

- Any team member may halt publication if factual integrity is doubtful.
- Restart requires explicit re-approval from Comms + Legal + Purple Manager.

## 9) Audit Requirements

- Log each major decision: evidence used, owner, approvers, rationale, expected effect, risk class.
- Maintain correction ledger with timestamps and evidence links.
- Preserve version history of all published messaging artifacts.

## 10) Success Metrics

- Trust index trend (weekly)
- Contradiction incidents (target: near zero)
- Crisis containment time (T1/T2)
- Correction acceptance rate
- Narrative share and favorability delta in target segments
